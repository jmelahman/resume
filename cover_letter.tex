To whom it may concern, \\

In college, I earned a degree in applied physics with a focus on computational astrophysics. I earned modest grades while working nearly 35 hours a week and graduating in 3 years. While in college, I was able to spend the summer doing computational physics research which I enjoyed much. \\

My second favorite (my first favorite paid for me to go to Kitt Peak, Arizona to do astrophysics on a research-grade telescope which is fairly hard to beat) and most memorable class was my Numerical Analysis in Matlab. I enjoyed the work so much, I turned all of my assignments into a Latex document I intended to add to my Github. The workbook is freely licensed and readily available online, so only at the request of my professor did I not. \\


The course came at a time in my life where I was starting to discover the open-source community. Perhaps to your displeasure, I completed the entire numerical analysis course with GNU Octave and SageMath. You may be pleased to know that the sometimes-subtle syntactic differences caused some pain in the process (and in one case, I was completely  unable to use Octave at all as the required feature had not been implemented). Point is, even if it is not something I am doing for work in the future, I would very much like to be able to contribute to these projects in the future. \\

Over the last year and a half, among many things, I have been triaging software crashes, unit test failures, as well as creating dashboards and custom Apps Scripts using Google’s Dremel SQL and Javascript which I think may translate nicely to this position.
Outside of work, I am largely interested in mathematics. At the moment, I have found a particular interest in Number Theory, particularly in regards to primes and the Riemann hypothesis. \\
